    Subsección Lección 12 - ¿Cuántos hay? (Parte 1)

    Objetivos de aprendizaje

    • [+++++++++++++++++]
    • Count collections of objects.
    • Say one number for each object.

    Estándares CCSS asociados.

    K.CC, K.CC.A.1, K.CC.B.4, K.CC.B.4.a, K.G.B, K.CC.B.4.a

    Momentos de la lección.

    Subsubsección Actividad 1 (15 mins)

    [+++++++++++++++++]
    The purpose of this lesson is for students to count collections of objects. The focus is saying one number for each object.

    Narrativa de la lección.

    [+++++++++++++++++]
    Students are introduced to a new routine called Questions About Us. In this version, they figure out how many students are in the classroom today. Students then count objects in different collections in a way that makes sense to them. The second activity is an optional activity that provides support in orally counting to 10. Throughout the section, observe students for the look-fors on the Unit 1, Sections A-D Checkpoint.

    Preguntas de reflexión.

    [+++++++++++++++++]
    Each lesson in this section includes an optional activity with additional support in developing counting concepts. What have you observed that indicates whether or not students may benefit from these activities?

    Subsubsección Calentamiento (10 mins)

    Tiempo recomendado.
    10 minutos
    Narrativa.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    The purpose of this warm-up is for students to experience part of the Questions About Us routine. Students continue to engage in this routine throughout the section, focused on answering “¿Cuántos estamos hoy aquí?”. In this activity students associate one number with one person as they count the students in the class. As students share answers to questions such as “¿Cómo podemos averiguar cuántos estamos hoy aquí?” and “¿Conté correctamente los estudiantes?” they are beginning to explain their reasoning and construct viable arguments (MP3).
    While the teacher counts the students in the class, students count along to practice the verbal count sequence.
    Lanzamiento.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    • “¿Cómo podemos averiguar cuántos estamos aquí?”
    • 30 seconds: quiet think time
    • Share responses.
    • Monitor for students who suggest touching and counting each student.
    Desarrollo de la actividad.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    • Count the students, saying one number for each student.
    • “¿Cuántos estamos hoy aquí?” // “How many of us are here today?”

    Calentamiento 38. Preguntas sobre nosotros: ¿Cuántos estamos hoy aquí?

    ¿Cuántos estamos hoy aquí?
    Solución.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    Sample response:
    • We can count all of the students.
    Síntesis de la actividad.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    • Bring 5 students to the front of the class.
    • Demonstrate counting the students incorrectly by saying 2 numbers for 1 student.
    • “¿Conté correctamente los estudiantes?”

    Subsubsección Actividad 1 (15 mins)

    Tiempo recomendado.
    15 minutos
    Narrativa.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    The purpose of this activity is for students to count their collection in a way that makes sense to them. The focus is on saying one number for each object. Most students should be given collections with 6–10 objects. Based on formative assessment data collected in previous sections, adjust the number of objects in collections for individual students. Students are provided with counting mats and 5-frames to help them accurately count or organize their collections. Students use appropriate tools strategically as they choose which tools help them count their collections (MP5).
    Collections can be created from classroom objects such as connecting cubes, two-color counters, pattern blocks, or buttons. Students can also bring in collections of objects to count from home. Collections of 6-10 objects will be used throughout this section.
    A counting mat is provided as a blackline master. Students will continue to use this mat throughout the year. Consider copying the mat on cardstock or laminating it and keeping it organized to be used repeatedly.
    Materiales.
    • Tableros de 5. Uno o dos para cada estudiante. Disponible en el libro de trabajo, o descargar acá
       42 
      external/blm/pdf-source/tablero-de-5.pdf
      .
    • Colecciones de objetos. Basado en los datos de evaluación formativa, cada estudiante necesita:
      • Colección de 1 a 5 objetos
      • Colección de 6 a 10 objetos
      • Colección de más de 10 objetos (opcional)
    • Tablero de conteo. Uno para cada estudiante. Disponible en el libro de trabajo, o descargar acá
       43 
      external/blm/pdf-source/contemosColecciones-tableroDeConteo-countingMat.pdf
      .
    Lanzamiento.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    • Display a collection of 6–10 objects.
    • “¿Cómo podemos descifrar cuántos objetos hay en esta colección?”
    • 30 seconds: quiet think time
    • Share responses.
    Desarrollo de la actividad.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    • Give each student a bag of objects.
    • Give students access to 5-frames and a counting mat.
    • “Descifren cuántos objetos hay en su colección. Usen las herramientas si les ayudan”
    • 2 minutes: independent work time
    • “Intercambien colecciones con un compañero. Descubran cuántos objetos hay en su nueva colección”
    • 2 minutes: independent work time
    • Monitor for students who say one number for each object.

    Actividad 39. Contemos colecciones.

    ¿Cuántos objetos hay en la colección?
    Tableros de 5 disponibles en el libro de trabajo, o descargar acá
     40 
    external/blm/pdf-source/tablero-de-5.pdf
    .
    Tableros de conteo disponibles en el libro de trabajo, o descargar acá
     41 
    external/blm/pdf-source/contemosColecciones-tableroDeConteo-countingMat.pdf
    .
    Solución.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    Students say one number for each object as they count.
    Para los estudiantes con dificultades.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    If students say more than one number for each object, consider asking:
    • “¿Cómo puede el tablero de conteo ayudarte a decir un número para cada objeto?”
    • If needed, prompt students to count as you move each object slowly from one side of the counting mat to the other.
    Students may also benefit from the optional activities in the next two lessons.
    Síntesis de la actividad.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    • Invite previously identified students to demonstrate how they counted their collections.
    • “¿Qué observan sobre la manera como ellos contaron?”
    • If needed, “Ellos decían un número al tocar cada objeto”
    • After each student shares, write or display the number and say, “Hay objetos en su colección”
    Desarrollo de lenguaje matemático.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    MLR8 Discussion Supports. Students who are working toward verbal output will benefit from additional opportunities to count aloud. Invite students to chorally repeat each count, in unison 1–2 times.
    Advances: Listening, Speaking
    Acceso a estudiantes con discapacidades.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    Action and Expression: Internalize Executive Functions. Invite students to verbalize their strategy for counting the objects in their collection before they begin. Students can speak quietly to themselves, or share with a partner.
    Supports accessibility for: Organization, Conceptual Processing, Language

    Subsubsección Actividad 2 (10 mins)

    Tiempo recomendado.
    10 minutos
    Narrativa.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    The purpose of this optional activity is for students to practice the verbal count sequence to 10. This activity is optional because it is an opportunity for extra practice that not all students may need. Based on formative assessment data and observation from previous sections and during the first activity, this activity will be helpful for students who are not yet saying the count sequence to 10. English learners in particular will benefit from this additional opportunity to practice the verbal count sequence.
    This activity can be used with a small-group or the whole class. Students who do not need this optional activity may benefit from additional time working in centers. Some students may benefit from working on the concepts in this optional activity more than one time. Consider incorporating counting and counting songs throughout the day and during transitions.
    Lanzamiento.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    • Groups of 2
    • “Voy a contar hasta 10”
    • Count to 10.
    Desarrollo de la actividad.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    • “Contemos juntos hasta 10”
    • Count to 10 all together.
    • “Contemos hasta 10 y aplaudamos al decir cada número”
    • Count to 10 and clap all together.
    • “Contemos hasta 10 y toquemos la mesa al decir cada número”
    • “Contemos hasta 10 y levantemos un dedo al decir cada número”
    • Count to 10 and put up each finger all together.

    Actividad 40. Contemos hasta 10 [Opcional].

    Contemos juntos hasta 10
    Solución.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    Students say the verbal count sequence to 10 and pair one gesture or movement with each number.
    Para los estudiantes con dificultades.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    If students “sing” the count sequence without separating each number word, prompt them to count slowly and match a gesture or movement with each number.
    Síntesis de la actividad.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    • “Jueguen por turnos con su compañero a contar hasta 10. Pueden aplaudir o tocar la mesa al decir cada número. También pueden inventar su propio movimiento para cada número”
    • 1 minute: partner discussion

    Subsubsección Actividad 3 (20 mins)

    Tiempo recomendado.
    20 minutos
    Narrativa.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    The purpose of this activity is for students to learn stage 3 of the Pattern Blocks center. In this center, students use a specified number of each pattern block to create with. While the written number is provided, students can use the images to determine how many pattern blocks they need. For example, 5 blue rhombuses are pictured next to the number “5”. Students can place pattern blocks on top of the images of rhombuses to determine how many they need, which gives students practice in creating groups with the same number. Students may also count to determine how many of each pattern block they need.
    After they participate in the center, students choose from any stage of previously introduced centers.
    • Connecting Cubes
    • Pattern Blocks
    • Geoblocks
    • Picture Books
    Students will choose from these centers throughout the section. Keep materials from these centers organized to use each day.
    Materiales.
    • Fichas geométricas
    • Instrucciones para el centro ‘Fichas geométricas: Consigue y construye’. Disponible en el libro de trabajo, o descargar acá
       45 
      external/blm/pdf-source/centro-fichasGeometricas-etapa3.pdf
      .
    • Materiales de los centros anteriores.
      • Centro: Fichas Geométricas, Etapa 1 y Etapa 2
      • Centro: Cubos Encajables, Etapa 1 y Etapa 2
      • Centro: Bloques sólidos geométricos, Etapa 1 y Etapa 2
      • Centro: Libros de imágenes, Etapa 1 y Etapa 2
    Lanzamiento.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    • Groups of 2
    • Display the student book.
    • “¿Qué observan? ¿Qué se preguntan?”
    • 30 seconds: quiet think time
    • 1 minute: partner discussion
    • Share and record responses.
    • “Vamos a aprender una nueva forma de trabajar en el centro ‘Fichas geométricas’. Se llama ‘Fichas geométricas: Consigue y construye’”
    • “Esta página les muestra cuáles fichas geométricas necesitan. Trabajen con su pareja para sacar todas las fichas geométricas que necesitan”
    • 3 minutes: partner work time
    • Monitor for students who place the pattern blocks on top of the images to determine how many pattern blocks they need.
    • Invite previously identified students to share how they determined which pattern blocks they needed.
    • “Ahora pueden usar sus fichas geométricas para crear lo que quieran. Pueden hacer un robot, un diseño o algo diferente”
    Desarrollo de la actividad.
    [+++++++++++++++++]
    • 3 minutes: partner work time
    • “Ahora pueden escoger otro centro o pueden seguir jugando ‘Fichas geométricas‘”
    • If students want to continue with stage 3 of Pattern Blocks, give them a copy of the blackline master with more pattern blocks they can get and build with.
    • Display the center choices in the student book.
    • Invite students to work at the center of their choice.
    • 10 minutes: center work time
    • If time, invite students to choose another center.
